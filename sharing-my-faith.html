Background

We wanted something to effectively communicate the good news of our Lord Jesus to our military friends and colleagues. The form of the tract is intended both to allure (we all want another medal!) and to speak of the valour & free gift through the cross.

The key element is that medals are earned whereas our Lord Jesus Christ freely gave Himself for us. Whilst there is much theology around evangelism we believe that our role is simply to lift up Christ and people will be drawn to Him (John 3:14-15). To that end we do not seek to give a point by point gospel message or a 'how to' guide- we want to 'put a stone in the shoe' such that when circumstances arise.

The Victorious Cross is built around the key verse about ...the word of God is alive and active. Sharper than any double-edged sword, it penetrates even to dividing soul and spirit, joints and marrow; it judges the thoughts and attitudes of the heart. (Hebrews 4:12) and
As the rain and the snow come down from heaven, and do not return to it without watering the earth and making it bud and flourish, so that it yields seed for the sower and bread for the eater, 11 so is my word that goes out from my mouth: It will not return to me empty, but will accomplish what I desire and achieve the purpose for which I sent it. (Isaiah 55:10-11). The tract expands on Romans 5:7-8:

Very rarely will anyone die for a righteous person, though for a good person someone might possibly dare to die. But God demonstrates his own love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us.
